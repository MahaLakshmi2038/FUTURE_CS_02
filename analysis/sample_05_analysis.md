# Sample 05 Analysis – Prize Giveaway Scam

## Analysis Summary

| Field | Observation |
|--------|-------------|
| Sample ID | 05 |
| Classification | Phishing |
| Risk Level | High |
| Attack Type | Prize Scam / Credential Theft |

---

# Objective

Analyze the email to determine whether it is a legitimate promotional message or a phishing attempt.

---

# Phishing Indicators

## 1. Unrealistic Reward

The email claims the recipient has won ₹10,00,000 without participating in any contest.

**Risk:** High

---

## 2. Request for Personal Information

The email asks for personal and banking details.

### Analysis

Legitimate organizations do not request sensitive information through unsolicited emails.

---

## 3. Suspicious Link

The link directs users to a non-official website.

---

## 4. Urgency

Recipients are pressured to respond within 24 hours.

---

## Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

| IOC Type | Value | Reason |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Subject | Congratulations! You've Won ₹10,00,000 | Too-good-to-be-true reward |
| URL | claim-your-reward.example | Suspicious website |
| Requested Information | Bank details | Sensitive information |
| Attack Technique | Prize Scam | Financial fraud |

---

# Final Classification

**Classification:** Phishing

**Risk Rating:** High

---

# Recommended Actions

- Ignore unsolicited prize notifications.
- Never share banking or personal information through email links.
- Verify promotional offers through official websites.
- Report the email as phishing.

---

# Analyst's Conclusion

This email is a classic prize scam designed to steal personal and financial information by exploiting excitement and urgency.
