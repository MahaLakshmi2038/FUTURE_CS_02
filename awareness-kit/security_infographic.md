# Security Infographic Content

## Think Before You Click!

### 8 Steps to Identify a Phishing Email

### 1. Verify the Sender
- Check the sender's email address.
- Look for misspelled or fake domains.

---

### 2. Don't Trust Urgency
Be cautious of messages claiming:

- Account suspension
- Immediate payment
- Prize expiration
- Emergency requests

---

### 3. Inspect Links
- Hover over links before clicking.
- Verify that the domain is legitimate.
- Avoid shortened or unfamiliar URLs.

---

### 4. Be Careful with Attachments
Never open unexpected:

- ZIP files
- EXE files
- Office documents requesting macros

---

### 5. Protect Your Credentials
Never enter passwords after clicking an email link.

Instead, visit the organization's official website directly.

---

### 6. Watch for Social Engineering
Attackers often exploit:

- Fear
- Curiosity
- Urgency
- Authority
- Excitement

---

### 7. Report Suspicious Emails
If something seems suspicious:

- Don't reply.
- Don't click links.
- Report it to your IT or Security Team.

---

### 8. Remember

**STOP → VERIFY → THINK → ACT**

Taking a few extra seconds can prevent phishing attacks, malware infections, and financial fraud.
