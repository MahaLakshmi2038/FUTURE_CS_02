# Employee Cybersecurity Awareness Guide

## Purpose

This guide provides practical recommendations to help employees recognize, avoid, and report phishing emails before they cause harm to the organization.

---

# What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack in which attackers impersonate trusted individuals or organizations to trick users into revealing sensitive information, downloading malware, or performing unauthorized actions.

Common targets include:

- Login credentials
- Banking information
- Personal information
- Company financial data

---

# Common Warning Signs

Be cautious if an email:

- Creates a sense of urgency.
- Requests passwords or confidential information.
- Contains unexpected attachments.
- Includes suspicious or shortened links.
- Claims your account will be suspended.
- Promises prizes, rewards, or unexpected refunds.
- Requests immediate financial transfers.
- Appears to come from a senior executive using an unusual email address.

---

# Safe Email Practices

Always:

- Verify the sender's email address.
- Hover over links before clicking.
- Download attachments only from trusted sources.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Use strong, unique passwords.
- Report suspicious emails immediately.

Never:

- Share passwords through email.
- Open unexpected attachments.
- Trust urgent requests without verification.
- Transfer money based solely on an email request.

---

# What To Do If You Receive a Phishing Email

1. Do not click links.
2. Do not download attachments.
3. Do not reply to the sender.
4. Report the email to your IT or Security team.
5. Delete or quarantine the email.
6. Change your password immediately if you entered your credentials.

---

# Key Takeaway

Cybersecurity is a shared responsibility.

Technology can block many phishing attempts, but informed users remain one of the strongest defenses against social engineering attacks.
