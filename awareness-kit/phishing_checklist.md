# 🛡️ How to Spot a Phishing Email

## Phishing Detection Checklist

Before clicking any link or opening any attachment, ask yourself the following questions:

---

## ✅ Sender Verification

☐ Is the sender's email address legitimate?

☐ Does the domain match the official organization?

☐ Is the display name different from the actual email address?

---

## ✅ Subject Line

☐ Does the email create unnecessary urgency?

☐ Does it threaten account suspension or financial loss?

☐ Does it promise unrealistic rewards?

---

## ✅ Email Content

☐ Are there spelling or grammatical mistakes?

☐ Does the message ask for passwords or sensitive information?

☐ Is the greeting generic (e.g., "Dear Customer")?

---

## ✅ Links

☐ Have you hovered over the link before clicking?

☐ Does the destination match the official website?

☐ Does the URL contain unusual words or numbers?

---

## ✅ Attachments

☐ Were you expecting this attachment?

☐ Is it a ZIP, EXE, or Office document requesting macros?

☐ Has it been scanned before opening?

---

## ✅ Requests

☐ Is someone asking for money?

☐ Is someone asking for banking information?

☐ Is someone asking you to bypass company procedures?

---

## ✅ If You Are Unsure

✔ Do not click anything.

✔ Verify using another communication channel.

✔ Report the email to your IT or Security Team.

✔ Delete or quarantine the email if confirmed malicious.

---

## Final Rule

> **Stop. Verify. Think. Then Act.**

A few seconds of verification can prevent credential theft, malware infections, and financial fraud.
